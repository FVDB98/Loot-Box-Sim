About

What...

What is a loot box?

In video games, a loot box (also called a loot crate or prize crate) is a consumable virtual item which can be redeemed to receive a randomised selection of further virtual items, or loot, ranging from simple customization options for a player's avatar or character to game-changing equipment such as weapons and armor. A loot box is typically a form of monetisation, with players either buying the boxes directly or receiving the boxes during play and later buying "keys" with which to redeem them.

Why...

The purpose of this website is to show the reality of loot boxes, and highlight to players how unlikely they will be to unlock the items they most desire. By showing just how much money one would have to spend to hopefully unlock certain items, the aim is to make players aware of how far their money will go - whether it's $10, $100, $1000, or even $10,000.

Not all loot box systems are bad, but some are predatory and the ability for children to spend large sums of money on mechanics that are illustrative of gambling is concerning.

How...

In recent affairs, companies like EA have been required to disclose the probability of unlocking certain items. Using these published numbers I have used javascript to create a basic simulator to unlock items. To reinforce, these simulators are in no way 100% accurate. They are mererly a tool for players and outsiders to gain perspective.
